Mission Briefings

Strategic Operations Schedule

INQUISITIVE

Step into the Signal Room as a cryptographer. Intercept enemy transmissions and solve wartime ciphers.

Day 1 - 0900 Hours

90 Minutes

View Details

Event 1 – The Signal Room

Role: Newly assigned cryptographers in a fortified communications bunker.

Briefing: “You are the unseen front line. Every cipher you break, every signal you intercept, is another step toward saving our forces. Fail — and they walk blind into the enemy’s guns.”

Objective: Crack intercepted enemy transmissions before they can be acted upon.

Task: Solve logic puzzles, wartime ciphers, and code-breaking challenges under time pressure.

Outcome:

  • Correct solutions reveal cipher fragments — numbers, symbols, words.
  • The final puzzle points to the movement of a mysterious classified cargo through hostile territory.
  • This decoded intel becomes the mission map for Event 2.

SQUABBLE

Take on the role of diplomatic operatives securing safe passage for the cargo decoded in Event 1. Negotiate, strategize, and protect the shipment.

Day 2 - 1000 Hours

120 Minutes

View Details

Event 2 – The Iron Route

Role: Diplomatic operatives securing safe passage for the “cargo” decoded in Event 1.

Briefing: “You must ensure the package’s delivery. No one must suspect its true nature. You will bargain, lie, or sacrifice — whatever is needed to clear its path.”

Objective: Protect the shipment without revealing its existence to neutral or hostile powers.

Task: Debate strategic routes, broker ceasefires, and negotiate alliances — each choice affects risk, time, and secrecy.

Outcome:

  • The chosen route is recorded in the mission log.
  • Intercepted messages from Event 1 resurface mid-negotiation, hinting at deeper connections.
  • The signed route agreement becomes part of the “technical delivery packet” for Event 3.

TBD

Take command as military engineers securing and containing the cargo from Event 2. Precision and care are essential.

Day 3 - 1200 Hours

150 Minutes

View Details

Event 3 – The Black Forge

Role: Military engineers tasked with securing and containing the “cargo” from Event 2.

Briefing: “The enemy compound you now hold is unstable. Mishandle it, and it will destroy you. Secure it to Command’s exact specifications — there can be no mistakes.”

Objective: Design a containment casing for an unstable, classified payload.

Task: Using provided materials, sketches, or models, build a casing that matches strict “classified technical specs” — secretly embedding cipher patterns from Event 1 and coordinates from Event 2.

Outcome:

  • Completed design is approved by High Command.
  • A technician quietly remarks: “Do you even know what you’ve made?”
  • First real hint that the events are connected.

Grand Reveal

Gather at the secure war council as the true consequences of your previous missions are revealed. Your moral choice will shape the outcome of the conflict.

Day 4 - 1300 Hours

90 Minutes

View Details

Event 4 – The Convergence Briefing

Setting: A secure underground war council. Lights dim. A single projector illuminates the table.

Reveals:

  • Cipher fragments from Event 1 were the targeting coordinates.
  • Route choices from Event 2 ensured the payload arrived at the strike zone.
  • Casing design from Event 3 was the warhead housing itself.

Truth: You have unknowingly built the deployment system for a weapon capable of ending the war in a single strike — but at the cost of annihilating an entire city.

Final Decision:

  • Strike immediately — ending the war but killing thousands.
  • Delay — risking the war dragging on, with unknown losses.
  • Sabotage — rendering the weapon unusable.
  • Redirect — fire into an uninhabited zone as a warning shot.

Intelligence Briefing

Frequently Asked Questions about Operation Trinity Veil

Operation Trinity Veil is a multi-stage immersive event testing logic, diplomacy, and engineering skills through cryptography, strategic negotiation, and classified payload tasks. Success requires precision and teamwork.

Participants include cryptographers, strategists, engineers, and enthusiasts in logic, diplomacy, or military simulation games. Each stage challenges a different skill set.

The event progresses through four main missions:

  • Event 1 – The Signal Room: Solve ciphers under time pressure.
  • Event 2 – The Iron Route: Negotiate and protect decoded cargo.
  • Event 3 – The Black Forge: Build a containment casing using secret intel.
  • Event 4 – Convergence Briefing: Face the moral choice that affects the outcome of the operation.

Teams register online for each stage. Registration closes before the first event, and late entries may not be accommodated. Each participant must provide a codename and skill specialization.

Minimum age is 16. Prior knowledge of logic puzzles, basic cryptography, or strategy games is recommended but not required. Teamwork is essential.
